## Projects

**OSHA Vision Auditor**  
A computer vision tool for workplace safety inspection that I built at a hackathon. It runs a YOLOv8 model to catch OSHA violations in images and video in real time, then writes up the violations with severity levels and suggested fixes. The backend is FastAPI, the frontend is Next.js, and Supabase handles data. The idea was to cut down the manual walkthroughs safety teams have to do.  
Python, PyTorch, YOLOv8, FastAPI, Next.js, Supabase

**Speciation Simulation in Astyanax mexicanus**  
A stochastic Monte Carlo simulation in C++ that models how the Mexican cavefish splits into separate species. I fed in population size, mutation rate, fitness, reproductive variability, and environmental pressure, then ran it across hundreds of randomized trials. Cave populations diverged in roughly 2,800 generations and surface populations in roughly 35,000, which lined up with what the literature reports. Done under faculty mentorship at Elgin Community College.  
C++, Monte Carlo methods, statistical modeling

**The Quest for Prime Numbers (UIC MURL Research)**  
Undergraduate research through UIC's MURL program under Professor Evangelos Kobotis. The work was about taking mathematical ideas and turning them into code that runs, then testing how different prime number algorithms hold up at large scales.  
C++, algorithm design, computational efficiency
